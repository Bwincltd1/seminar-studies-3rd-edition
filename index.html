Description

South Africa: The Rise and Fall of Apartheid explore South Africa’s past from 1948 to the present day, covering the implementation of an authoritarian apartheid policy when the Nationalists came to power, its increasing resistance in the 1970s and 1980s, its subsequent downfall in the 1990s, and its legacy up to the present.

Fully revised, the third edition includes:

  • New research on the effects of apartheid, including the social and cultural implications of urbanization as Africans were forced out of rural areas
  • Review of recent political and economic problems rooted in the apartheid regime, including increased unemployment and the emergence of opposition political parties such as the Economic Freedom Fighters
  • An updated Read more segment, reflecting the dramatically increased availability of online materials
  • An extended collection of primary-source records, offering insight into the minds of those who practiced and battled apartheid.

Illustrated with images, maps, and statistics, including a chronology of events, glossary and Who’s Who of key personalities, this critical text offers students an up-to-date, simple and concise introduction to South Africa’s philosophy and practice of apartheid.
